Selected Humanitarian Case Studies

Examples of complex assignments combining finance, programme delivery, branch coordination, donor accountability and field-level implementation.

Ukraine response

Austrian Red Cross — Integrated Master Budget

Supported a consolidated multi-donor master budget of approximately €11M over three years, with €7M concentrated in the first two years. The structure integrated operational components such as seasonal CVA, trainings and branch support into one financial management framework.

  • • Branch support: Donetsk, Zaporizhzhia, Chernihiv, Lviv, Zakarpattia and Chernivtsi.
  • • Main donors: Neighbour in Need and Austrian Development Agency.
  • • Team interface: one officer directly supervised plus two URCS finance colleagues.

CVA / Poland

German Red Cross — Ukraine Refugee Response

Supported a €1.6M CVA operation for 300 vulnerable families, prioritising older couples with chronic diseases, single mothers with two or more children and people with chronic health conditions.

  • • RedRose CVA platform procurement and operational setup.
  • • 10–15 volunteers mobilised per distribution cycle.
  • • CVA trainings for 40–60 volunteers per session in larger branch hubs.

Sudan / EU food security

Spanish Red Cross — Food Security, WASH & Livelihoods

Managed programme follow-up, EU reporting, procurement processes, audits and closure of a multi-sector operation in Red Sea State combining food security, livelihoods, WASH, gender and community capacity building.

  • • €1.2M EU-funded action over a programme extended to three years.
  • • 28 rural communities in Jebet al Maaden.
  • • Additional Diputación de Jaén contribution for complementary activities.

Professional Approach

I bring a finance-and-operations profile shaped by field realities: liquidity constraints, documentation pressure, donor deadlines, branch-level capacity gaps, emergency implementation and the need to translate complex budgets into decisions that programme teams can actually use.

My strongest contribution is the ability to connect financial control with operational delivery. I understand budgets, but also procurement bottlenecks, volunteer mobilisation, partner constraints, supporting documentation, programme indicators, donor compliance and the practical pressure of keeping activities moving in unstable environments.

I am particularly interested in roles where humanitarian finance is not treated as a back-office function, but as a management tool for quality implementation, accountability and strategic decision-making.
